Newton's Laws

Newton's laws of motion are three fundamental laws of physics that describe the relationship between an object's motion and the forces acting on it:

  1. An object will remain at rest or in uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by an external force.
  2. The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass (F = ma).
  3. For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Law of Momentum

The law of momentum conservation states that the total momentum of a closed system remains constant over time, provided no external forces are acting on it.
